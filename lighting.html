Lighting & Atmosphere

This section presents the lighting work I handled for the project. The goal was to create a dark horror atmosphere while keeping important gameplay elements readable for the player.

Interactive Lighting Feedback

These indicator lights are linked to the GPE charging system. When the player activates a GPE, the progress LEDs light up gradually, and once the GPE is fully charged, the bulbs turn green to show the player that the GPE is 100% charged.

Popcorn Machine: the machine lighting turns on to show that the objective has been activated.

Spot Light: the spotlight creates a strong focused beam, making the activated area more visible.

Door: the door opens once the charge reaches 100%.

How it works

Light feedback is handled by the GPELoad system. When the GPE receives power, the script updates a set of progress LEDs and their emissive materials. When the GPE reaches 100% charge, a Unity event is triggered. Simultaneously, the script changes the color of the LED and the emissive shader to green, instantly indicating to the player that the GPE is fully charged.

If the GPE is not powered, it automatically discharges after a certain time. When the stored energy reaches zero, the system triggers the discharge event and resets the light components and emissive materials to their original colors.

General Lighting & Atmosphere

This video shows the general lighting mood of the level during navigation.

General lighting pass: dark corridors, warm practical lights, visible fog and strong flashlight contrast.

Atmosphere Breakdown

The scene uses a very dark base lighting setup with localized warm lights to create contrast and guide the eye. The fog makes the flashlight beam visible, which adds depth and reinforces the horror atmosphere. The result feels heavy, abandoned and cinematic, while still giving the player enough visual information to navigate the environment.
