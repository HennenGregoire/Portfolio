Lighting & Atmosphere

This section presents the lighting work I handled for the project. The goal was to create a dark horror atmosphere while keeping important gameplay elements readable for the player.

Interactive Lighting Feedback

These lighting groups are connected to the GPE charge system. When the player powers a GPE, progress LEDs turn on step by step, and once the GPE reaches full charge, the related lights activate to confirm the objective state.

Popcorn Machine: the machine lighting turns on to show that the objective has been activated.

Spot Light: the spotlight creates a strong focused beam, making the activated area more visible.

Door: the door lighting changes state to clearly communicate progression and guide the player forward.

How it works

The lighting feedback is driven by the GPELoad system. As the GPE receives energy, the script updates a set of progress LEDs and their emissive materials. When the GPE reaches 100% charge, a UnityEvent is triggered, allowing the related gameplay lights to activate. At the same time, the script changes the LED light color and emissive shader color to green, making the fully charged state instantly readable for the player.

If the GPE is not maintained, it automatically discharges over time. When the stored energy reaches zero, the system triggers the discharge event and resets both the Light components and emissive materials to their original colors.

General lighting pass: dark corridors, warm practical lights, visible fog and strong flashlight contrast.

Atmosphere Breakdown

The scene uses a very dark base lighting setup with localized warm lights to create contrast and guide the eye. The fog makes the flashlight beam visible, which adds depth and reinforces the horror atmosphere. The result feels heavy, abandoned and cinematic, while still giving the player enough visual information to navigate the environment.
